Meet Jesse Maina

Jesse Maina is a data engineer with a Master’s in Data Science & Analytics and a Bachelor’s in Informatics. His work is rooted in leveraging machine learning to transform education and healthcare systems. Beyond the technical realm, Jesse is a passionate storyteller and advocate for youth empowerment. He is a dynamic voice in education reform and youth empowerment. Through Friends of Education, he has transformed personal challenges into a mission to make quality education accessible to marginalized students. What began as a one-student sponsorship has grown into a grassroots initiative supporting multiple learners across Kenya.

As the visionary behind The Jesse Show, a youtube platform for storytelling, interviews, and social dialogue, Jesse curates conversations that spotlight real stories—from grassroots activism to global innovation. The show is a platform for underrepresented voices, with a focus on education, diaspora leadership, and civic responsibility.

He is also the author of A Played Nation, a powerful memoir that weaves personal narrative with political critique. The book reflects on Kenya’s 2022 elections and explores the intersections of youth, democracy, and identity. Blending memoir with activism, A Played Nation challenges readers to consider how political systems shape personal destinies.

His work across media, education, and literature is driven by a deep belief: that access, opportunity, and storytelling can change lives.
